# Artifact Review

Use this reference when reviewing produced artifacts such as slide decks, reports, screenshots, demos, canvases, exported documents, diagrams, or shareable deliverables.

## Review Focus

1. Identify artifact intent:
   - audience and decision the artifact should support
   - expected format and delivery context
   - source material or brand constraints
2. Check substance:
   - claims are supported or clearly framed as assumptions
   - narrative order makes the decision or message easy to follow
   - important caveats are visible
   - data, screenshots, diagrams, and examples match the text
3. Check presentation:
   - hierarchy, alignment, spacing, and density are coherent
   - repeated elements are consistent
   - visual choices serve the artifact's purpose
   - labels, captions, legends, and callouts are readable
4. Check delivery readiness:
   - exported format opens correctly when verifiable
   - links, embedded assets, and references are intact
   - any known limitations are called out

## Output

- Findings first, with slide/page/section/screen references when available.
- Separate blocking content issues from visual polish.
- Summarize readiness and the smallest correction path.

## Guardrails

- Do not redesign or recreate the artifact unless asked or as part of an explicit review-fix loop.
- Do not impose marketing-style polish on operational artifacts.
- Do not ignore factual support just because the artifact looks polished.
